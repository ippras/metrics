# Reynolds–Weir–Cockerham distance

$$D_W = -\ln(1 - \theta)$$

## Abbreviations

- $D_W$

## Description

The Reynolds–Weir–Cockerham distance between populations $x$ and $y$ is defined by the formula above, where $\theta$ is their co-ancestry coefficient estimated as $\frac{a}{a+b}$. Here $a$ is the variance between populations $x$ and $y$, and $b$ is the variance within them. If the sample size is large, then $D_W$ is close to the Latter F-statistics distance.
