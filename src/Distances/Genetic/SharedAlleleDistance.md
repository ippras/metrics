# Shared allele distance

$$1 - \frac{SA(x, y)}{SA(x) + SA(y)}$$

## Abbreviations

- $D_{SA}$

## Description

The shared allele distance between individuals $a$ and $b$ is $1 - SA(a, b)$; for populations $x$ and $y$ it is defined by the formula above, where $SA(a, b)$ denotes the number of shared alleles summed over all $n$ loci and divided by $2n$, while $SA(x)$, $SA(y)$, and $SA(x, y)$ are $SA(a, b)$ averaged over all pairs $(a, b)$ with individuals $a, b$ being in populations $x, y$, respectively.
