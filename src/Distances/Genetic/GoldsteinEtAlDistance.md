# Goldstein et al. distance

$$(\delta \mu)^2 = \frac{1}{n} \sum (ix_{ij} - iy_{ij})^2$$

## Abbreviations

- $(\delta \mu)^2$

## Description

The Goldstein et al. distance between populations $x$ and $y$ is defined by the formula above. It is the loci-averaged value $(\delta \mu)^2 = (\mu(x)_j - \mu(y)_j)^2$, where $\mu(z)_j = \sum_i i z_{ij}$ is the mean number of repeats of allele at the $j$-th (microsatellite) locus in population $z$.
